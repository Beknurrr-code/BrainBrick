## Slide 2: Problem Statement
*   **Target SDGs**: SDG 4 (Quality Education) & SDG 9 (Innovation & Infrastructure)
*   **The Identified Gap**: Current robotics education is hindered by high entry barriers. Coding complex behaviors (computer vision, logical reasoning) takes months to learn. There is no seamless "bridge" between affordable toys (LEGO) and cutting-edge Agentic AI.

## Slide 3: Solution Overview
*   **Strategic Pivot**: Evolving from a low-cost DIY hardware provider (Stage 1) to a **Universal AI-Native Operating System** (Stage 2). 
*   **Core Concept**: BrainBricks is now hardware-agnostic, capable of turning any educational robot into an "Agentic Twin".
*   **Unique Value Proposition**: Users can "forge" complex neural behaviors in the 3D Anvil and deploy them instantly. This allows us to scale across existing global hardware ecosystems (LEGO, Arduino, ESP32).
*   **Approach**: Hybrid React/Node.js architecture with Gemini Expert Reasoning.

## Slide 4: Market Research & Business Viability
*   **Target Users**: STEM students, hobbyists, and rapid prototype engineers.
*   **Market Scope**: The global EdTech and DIY Robotics market (projected to reach $3B+ by 2030).
*   **Competition**: Scratch/Python (too manual), high-end industrial AI (too expensive).
*   **Sustainability**: Subscription-based "Neural Tiers" for cloud-AI nodes and a marketplace for AI-generated robot "blueprints".

## Slide 5: Prototype Progress & Pivot Rationale
*   **Current Stage**: Functional Hybrid MVP (Stage 2).
*   **Pivot Achievement**: Transitioned from DIY electronics focus to a full-stack **3D Forge & OS** that supports industry-standard platforms like LEGO Spike Prime. This ensures 10x faster market adoption by leveraging existing classroom equipment.
*   **Implemented Features**: 3D Builder, Real-time WebSocket Control, AI Vision/Voice link, and the WebBluetooth LEGO Driver.
*   **Next Steps**: Multi-agent collaborative missions and expanding the Forge Marketplace.
